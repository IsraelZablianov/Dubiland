# Handbook Reading Ladder — 10 Books (Hebrew: סולם קריאה לספרונים)

## Learning Objective
- Build a single Hebrew literacy progression across 10 interactive handbooks for ages 3-7.
- Ensure each book teaches a measurable reading target in sequence: print awareness -> letter+nikud decoding -> syllable blending -> word reading -> phrase/sentence fluency -> early text-dependent comprehension.
- Keep all early-reader text decodable and audio-supported, with explicit nikud policy per stage.

## Curriculum Position
- This document is the reading backbone for the handbook program.
- It sits after letter-foundation games (`letter-tracing-trail`, `letter-sound-match`, `letter-sky-catcher`) and before broad leveled-story scale.
- It operationalizes `docs/games/handbook-literacy-interaction-framework.md` into a concrete 10-book sequence and launch priorities.

## Target Age Range
- Full ladder: 3-7
- Reading-heavy core: 5-7

## Decodable Text and Vocabulary Progression by Age Group

### Ages 3-4 (Books 1-3)
- Primary literacy goal: oral language and print awareness, not independent decoding.
- Decodable policy:
  - Narrator models fully pointed anchor words.
  - Child tasks rely on first-sound matching, letter discrimination, and syllable hearing.
  - No performance scoring on independent pointed-word reading.
- Vocabulary profile:
  - Concrete, high-image nouns and action verbs from familiar worlds (garden, animals, home, movement).
  - 8-12 new target words per book, with repetition >=4 exposures per session.

### Ages 5-6 (Books 4-6)
- Primary literacy goal: accurate decoding of fully pointed CV/CVC words and short clauses.
- Decodable policy:
  - Text remains fully pointed in child-evaluated reading checkpoints.
  - Grapheme progression is controlled: high-frequency letters first, then confusables and final forms.
  - At least 85% of checkpoint tokens are decodable from already-taught correspondences.
- Vocabulary profile:
  - 12-18 target words per book in reusable semantic sets (school, market, route, objects).
  - Function words introduced in repeated sentence frames.

### Ages 6-7 (Books 7-10)
- Primary literacy goal: phrase/sentence fluency, comprehension, and bridge toward reduced pointing.
- Decodable policy:
  - Begin mostly pointed, then gradually reduce pointing on previously mastered words.
  - Introduce shva patterns and morphology-light contrasts only after stable word decoding.
  - Keep text-dependent checks; no "guess from picture" pass paths.
- Vocabulary profile:
  - 18-26 target words per book with semantic families and early root-pattern clustering.
  - Repeated phrase frames for fluency plus controlled novelty for transfer.

## First 3 Prioritized Launch Books (One Per Age Group)

### Priority A (Age 3-4): Book 1 — מיקה בגן הקולות
- Hebrew reading objective:
  - Track print right-to-left and connect 6 anchor letters to their initial sounds through repeated story prompts.
- Nikud/phonics expectations:
  - Child hears fully pointed model words; no independent nikud production expected.
  - Targeted phonological awareness: initial sound identification in spoken words.
- Target sentence complexity:
  - Narration lines of 2-4 words, mostly one clause.
  - Interaction prompts are one action per line.
- Comprehension checks:
  - 1 literal prompt every 2-3 pages ("מי מצא את ה...?", "איפה מיקה עומדת?").
  - 2-choice responses with audio replay and hint.

### Priority B (Age 5-6): Book 4 — יואב ומפת האותיות
- Hebrew reading objective:
  - Decode fully pointed CV syllables and 2-4 letter words with >=80% first/second-attempt accuracy.
- Nikud/phonics expectations:
  - Core nikud set: patah, kamatz, segol, hiriq in fully pointed targets.
  - Explicit contrast tasks for confusable letters (for example ב/כ, ד/ר) in controlled frequency.
- Target sentence complexity:
  - 3-5 word single-clause sentences.
  - Repeated sentence frames with one changing decodable token.
- Comprehension checks:
  - Literal text-first checks after decoding ("מה יואב קרא במפה?").
  - Require child to read target word/phrase before answering.

### Priority C (Age 6-7): Book 7 — תמר ומגדל המילים
- Hebrew reading objective:
  - Read short pointed phrases fluently, then answer literal/sequence questions from the decoded text.
- Nikud/phonics expectations:
  - 80-90% pointed text in early pages, with selective reduction on mastered high-frequency words in late pages.
  - Final forms and introductory shva patterns appear only in pre-taught forms.
- Target sentence complexity:
  - 5-8 words, one connector (for example ואז, כי) and one temporal marker.
  - One short two-clause sentence per page cluster after warm-up pages.
- Comprehension checks:
  - Literal and order checks ("מה קרה קודם? מה קרה אחר כך?") tied to read sentence.
  - At least one text-evidence prompt per session where child taps the sentence fragment that supports the answer.

## Pre-Literate UX Baseline (Mandatory)
- Every child-facing instruction has adjacent `▶` replay icon (44px+).
- Gameplay controls stay icon-first: replay (`▶`), retry (`↻`), hint (`💡`), continue (`→`) with audio cues.
- No text-only action labels in child gameplay UI.
- Validation is action-triggered; no separate `check`/`test` button.

## Audio and i18n Requirements
- All handbook text and prompts remain i18n-keyed; no hardcoded Hebrew strings in runtime.
- Required reading key family pattern:
  - `common.handbooks.<bookSlug>.meta.*`
  - `common.handbooks.<bookSlug>.scriptPackage.{narration,prompts,hints,retry,praise}.*`
  - `common.handbooks.<bookSlug>.sentenceBank.*`
  - `common.handbooks.<bookSlug>.interactions.<interactionId>.{prompt,hint,success,retry}`
  - `common.handbooks.<bookSlug>.readingProgression.*` (for reading-stage books)
  - `common.parentDashboard.handbooks.<bookSlug>.*`
- Launch slugs for first production trio:
  - `mikaSoundGarden` (Book 1)
  - `yoavLetterMap` (Book 4)
  - `tamarWordTower` (Book 7)
- Audio behavior:
  - Instruction replay is exact same clip as original prompt.
  - Decoding model audio must be pace-controlled for beginner readers.

## Parent Visibility

  - hint usage trend
  - top confusion contrasts (letters/nikud/patterns)
  - readiness signal for next book in the ladder
