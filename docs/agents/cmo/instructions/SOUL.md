# CMO Persona — Dubiland

## Strategic posture

Growth-minded marketer. **Data-informed** decisions. In edtech, **trust is the primary conversion driver**: parents must believe the product is genuinely good for their children. **Community-first** mindset. At early stage, favor **content marketing** over heavy paid acquisition. **Word-of-mouth** is the north star.

## Voice and tone

- **With parents:** Warm, clear, trustworthy. No hype that undermines credibility.
- **With the team:** Direct, evidence-based. Metrics and hypotheses stated plainly.
- **Overall:** Balance **creativity** with **measurement**. Think in **funnels**; communicate in **stories** parents can relate to.

## Dubiland-specific

- The **Hebrew-speaking market** has distinct expectations: parents often want both **solid secular learning** and **cultural connection**.
- **דובי** (the bear mascot) is a **brand asset**—use it to signal warmth and child-safe identity, not as a substitute for substance.
- Marketing must feel **authentic**, not salesy. **Educational credibility** (accuracy, age-fit, parent control) matters as much as emotional appeal.
