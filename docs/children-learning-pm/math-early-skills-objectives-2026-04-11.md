# Math and Early-Skills Objective Refresh (Ages 3-7)

Date: 2026-04-11  
Issue: [DUB-676](/DUB/issues/DUB-676)

## Progression Review (Current State)

| Age Band | Current Math Coverage | Strength | Gap Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3-4 | `counting-picnic`, `shape-safari`, `color-garden` | Strong concrete counting + categorization foundations | Needs broader pattern/sequence exposure beyond single-variable sort tasks |
| 5-6 | `more-or-less-market` + Level 2 in shapes/colors | Strong quantity comparison and guided rule introduction | Missing explicit number composition/decomposition and clock-time foundations |
| 6-7 | `number-line-jumps` + advanced levels in existing games | Strong early addition strategy | Missing subtraction companion and everyday math transfer (time/routines) |

## New Learning Objectives Added in This Heartbeat

1. Subtraction strategy (`within 10/20`) with misconception-aware remediation.
2. Number composition/decomposition (`5`, `10`, `20`) with alternate-solution flexibility.
3. Sequencing + time anchors (hour/half-hour) for school-readiness routines.

## Improvement Notes for Existing Specs

### Counting Picnic
- Add one micro-block of rapid subitizing (`1-4`) before counting-by-one prompts for advanced 4-5 children.
- Keep counting objective primary; subitizing should remain optional/adaptive.

### More or Less Market
- Keep `equal` unlock gate strict until stable `more/less` mastery.
- Add a parent-facing "close-comparison" metric (difference of 1) as a readiness signal for subtraction lanes.

### Number Line Jumps
- Add explicit handoff recommendation to `Subtraction Street` when child is stable in bridge-to-10 addition.
- Track cross-game transition readiness in parent dashboard summaries.

### Shape Safari / Color Garden (Ages 6-7 stretch)
- Reserve 6-7 extension for rule-combination reasoning only after single-variable mastery.
- Avoid using these as primary math depth lanes for older children; treat as reinforcement.

## Next Wave Game Ideas (P1 Candidate Backlog)

1. **Pattern Train** (ages 4-6): pattern continuation and rule prediction with visual/audio cues.
2. **Measure and Match** (ages 6-7): longer/shorter, heavier/lighter, fuller/emptier with concrete objects.
3. **Money Market Mini** (ages 6-7): coin recognition and simple exchange stories in familiar Israeli contexts.

## Priority Decision

For the next math cycle, delivery should stay focused on:
1. Subtraction depth,
2. Number bonds/place-value bridge,
3. Time/routine transfer,

before expanding into additional breadth-only discovery games.
