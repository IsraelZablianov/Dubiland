## Age-Band Outcomes (What a Child Should Be Able To Do)

### Ages 3-4
**After 1 week (4-6 short sessions):**
- Count sets up to 5 with one-to-one matching in guided tasks.
- Identify at least 6 high-frequency Hebrew letters in game contexts.
- Sort by basic color/shape with audio prompts.

**After 1 month (16-24 short sessions):**
- Count sets up to 10 and compare small sets (`more/less`) with support.
- Trace 8-12 Hebrew letters with reduced visual scaffolds.
- Follow simple 2-step audio instruction chains in familiar mechanics.

### Ages 5-6
**After 1 week:**
- Map 12-15 letter-sound pairs, including common confusables.
- Decode very short pointed syllables/words in guided frames.
- Compare quantities to ~20 and perform simple number-line jumps.

**After 1 month:**
- Decode 20-30 pointed words and short phrases with reduced hints.
- Complete beginner comprehension checks (literal retrieval) in guided stories.
- Solve addition within 10 using visual supports.

### Ages 6-7
**After 1 week:**
- Read short pointed phrases/sentences with audio support.
- Recognize a starter bank of frequent words automatically.
- Apply number-line addition up to ~20 with support.

**After 1 month:**
- Read 4-6 sentence decodable passages and answer literal comprehension prompts.
- Apply final forms in reading transfer tasks and use basic root-family cues.
- Solve mixed add/subtract tasks within 20 with visual scaffolds.

## Hard Gaps (Zero Dedicated Games Today, Especially for 5-7)
- Subtraction strategies and fact fluency (`within 10/20`): no dedicated game.
- Place value (`ones/tens`) and number composition/decomposition: no dedicated game.
- Measurement/time/money data interpretation: no dedicated game.
- Writing/encoding lane (from reading to spelling/writing production): no dedicated game.
- Listening comprehension/inference (beyond literal recall): no dedicated game.
- Multi-step logic/problem-solving puzzles for older children (6-7): no dedicated game.

## Benchmark Comparison (Depth vs Major Platforms)

### Khan Academy Kids
Source: https://www.khanacademy.org/kids and https://khankids.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/360014856151-Learning-Topics-Using-Khan-Academy-Kids-in-Educational-Settings
- Strength over Dubiland: broader scope (literacy, language, math, SEL, executive function, motor, creative expression) with adaptive sequencing and large content volume.
- Dubiland implication: keep Hebrew-native advantage, but expand beyond current literacy+foundational numeracy depth for ages 6-7.

### Lingokids
Source: https://lingokids.com/playlearning
- Strength over Dubiland: explicit breadth and scale ("600+ objectives", "1,200+ activities"), plus life-skill framing and highly varied content formats.
- Dubiland implication: for retention and mastery, add more mechanics per concept (same objective, varied play loop).

### HOMER (Begin)
Source: https://www.beginlearning.com/parent-resources/learn-to-read/
- Strength over Dubiland: explicit step-by-step literacy pathway and stronger parent-facing efficacy narrative.
- Dubiland implication: make progression and mastery checkpoints visible to parents, not just game completion counts.

## Actionable Recommendations (Task-Ready Backlog)

### P0 (Next 2-3 Specs)
1. **Subtraction Street (ages 6-7)**  
   Learning: subtraction as distance/backward movement on number line (`within 20`).  
   Mechanic: drag/jump backward with immediate action-based validation.  
   Success metric: 80% first-attempt accuracy on level-calibrated sets after 3 sessions.

2. **Build-10 Workshop (ages 5-7)**  
   Learning: number bonds and composition/decomposition (`5, 10, 20`).  
   Mechanic: snap manipulatives into target frames (ten-frames + part-part-whole).  
   Success metric: child forms target totals with at least two valid decompositions.

3. **Time-and-Routine Builder (ages 6-7)**  
   Learning: sequencing + clock basics (`hour/half-hour`) in child routines.  
   Mechanic: drag daily activities to clock anchors and validate by action.  
   Success metric: accurate ordering and time matching in mixed routine sets.

### P1 (Next Wave)
4. **Listen-and-Think Stories (ages 5-7)**  
   Learning: listening comprehension from literal -> inferential prompts.  
   Mechanic: short narrated scenes with picture-choice answers and hint ladder.

5. **Spell-and-Send (ages 6-7)**  
   Learning: transition from decoding to encoding (word spelling and short sentence writing with nikud support fade).  
   Mechanic: tap/drag keyboard tiles with guided audio prompts.

6. **Logic Quest: Rule Paths (ages 6-7)**  
   Learning: executive function (pattern, sequencing, flexible rule switching).  
   Mechanic: path puzzles with one-variable then two-variable rule shifts.

## Priority Decision
For educational depth in the 5-7 band, Dubiland should prioritize **Subtraction + Number Composition + Writing/Encoding** before adding additional early-years discovery games.
